311 Ticketing and Tree Health in the District of Columbia

In this project, completed during my General Assembly Data Analytics course, I investigated a hypothesis that community engagement in tree maintenance (measured by 311 tickets filed) and overall tree health are positively correlated. While geospatially analyzing the data, it was suspected that significant inconsistencies existed in the ways that inspectors measured and assigned tree health. As such, speculating on a relationship between tree health and any other quantitative variable would be unreliable.

It was recommended that more quantitative metrics be used to assess tree health, as opposed to the subjectivity of Excellent/Good/Fair/Poor/Dead.

Sources:
District of Columbia 311 Ticket Filing Data (2017-2019) , D.C. Office of the Chief Technology Officer
Urban Forestry Street Trees Database, D.C. Office of the Chief Technology Officer

Key Fuctions:
Data cleaning performed in PostgreSQL
Tableau Elements: Geospatial, Reference Bands, Categorical Variables, Storyboarding
